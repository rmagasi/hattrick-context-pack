# Position Contributions Reference

Full per-position skill contributions sourced from the Unwritten Manual #15 (LA-Alpa, HT Global English Forum thread 17665541) and verified against HT Organizer absolute values.

These are the percentages of each player's skill that contributes to each match rating sector when played in a given position with a given order.

---

## Goalkeeper

**GK** uses:
- 87% of GK skill → Central Defence
- 61% of GK skill → Side Defence (each side)
- 35% of Defending skill → Central Defence
- 25% of Defending skill → Side Defence (each side)

---

## Central Defenders

**CD (Normal)** uses:
- 100% Defending → Central Defence
- 26% Defending → Side Defence
- 25% Playmaking → Midfield

**RCD (Right Central Defender Normal)** uses:
- 100% Defending → Central Defence
- 52% Defending → Right Side Defence
- 0% Defending → Left Side Defence
- 25% Playmaking → Midfield

**LCD (Left Central Defender Normal)** uses:
- 100% Defending → Central Defence
- 52% Defending → Left Side Defence
- 0% Defending → Right Side Defence
- 25% Playmaking → Midfield

**CDO (Central Defender Offensive)** uses:
- 73% Defending → Central Defence
- 20% Defending → Side Defence (each side)
- 40% Playmaking → Midfield

**CDTW (Central Defender Towards Wing)** uses:
- 67% Defending → Central Defence
- 81% Defending → Side Defence (the side it's pointing to)
- 26% Winger → Side Attack (the side it's pointing to)
- 15% Playmaking → Midfield

## Wingers

**WO (Winger Offensive)** uses:

- 30% Playmaking → Midfield

- 22% Defending → Side Defence

- 29% Passing → Side Attack

**WD (Winger Defensive)** uses:
- 69% Winger → Side Attack
- 30% Playmaking → Midfield
- 25% Defending → Central Defence
- 61% Defending → Side Defence
- 21% Passing → Side Attack
- 5% Passing → Central Attack

**WTM (Winger Towards Middle)** uses:
- 74% Winger → Side Attack
- 55% Playmaking → Midfield
- 15% Passing → Side Attack
- 16% Passing → Central Attack
- 25% Defending → Central Defence
- 29% Defending → Side Defence

**IMO (Inner Midfielder Offensive)** uses:

**IMTW (Inner Midfielder Towards Wing)** uses:

## Forwards

**FW (Forward Normal)** uses:
- 100% Scoring → Central Attack
- 27% Scoring → Side Attack
- 33% Passing → Central Attack
- 14% Passing → Side Attack
- 24% Winger → Side Attack
- 25% Playmaking → Midfield

**FTW (Forward Towards Wing)** uses:
- 66% Scoring → Central Attack
- 51% Scoring → respective Side Attack (the side it points to)
- 19% Scoring → far Side Attack
- 23% Passing → Central Attack
- 21% Passing → respective Side Attack
- 6% Passing → far Side Attack
- 64% Winger → respective Side Attack
- 21% Winger → far Side Attack
- 15% Playmaking → Midfield

**DF (Defensive Forward)** uses:
- 56% Scoring → Central Attack
- 13% Scoring → Side Attack
- 35% Playmaking → Midfield
- 53% Passing → Central Attack
- 31% Passing → Side Attack
- 13% Winger → Side Attack

**TDF (Technical Defensive Forward)** — same as DF but:
- 41% Passing → Side Attack (instead of 31%)

This is the contribution boost that makes a Technical-spec'd DF distinctive for wing creation.

## Agglomeration Penalties (when fielding 2–3 in same central compartment)

All skills of the players in that compartment are reduced:

| Players in same central compartment | Penalty per player |
|---|---|
| 2 Central Defenders | −3.6% |
| 3 Central Defenders | −10% |
| 2 Inner Midfielders | −6.5% |
| 3 Inner Midfielders | −17.5% |
| 2 Forwards | −5.5% |
| 3 Forwards | −13.5% |

Also, **special-spec overcrowding penalty** for fielding 2-3 of the same combination of:
- Technical Defensive Forwards (TDF)
- Powerful Normal Forwards (PNF)
- Powerful Defensive Inner Midfielders (PDIM, "Sitting Midfielder")

Stacking penalty: **−4% each for 2**, **−7.5% each for 3**.

## Individual orders - qualitative effects per position (Rules 03)

The rulebook describes individual orders in plain-language deltas rather than fixed percentages, the formulas in this pack already encode them numerically per role (DF, FTW, AIM, etc.), this section is the human-readable summary so AI can map a user question like "what does a defensive winger do?" to the right mechanic.

**Wing back (WB)**
- *Offensive*, somewhat more Winger, somewhat more Playmaking, less Defending.
- *Defensive*, somewhat more Defending, less Winger, somewhat less Playmaking.
- *Toward middle*, much more central Defending, less side Defending, much less Winger, somewhat more Playmaking.

**Central defender (CD)**
- *Offensive*, more Playmaking, much less Defending.
- *Toward wing*, much more side Defending, much less central Defending, some Winger, somewhat less Playmaking.

**Winger (W)**
- *Offensive*, more Winger, somewhat more Passing, less Defending, less Playmaking.
- *Defensive*, much more Defending, less Winger, less Playmaking, somewhat less Passing.
- *Toward middle*, somewhat more Playmaking, less Winger, somewhat more central Defending, somewhat less side Defending, somewhat more central Passing, less side Passing.

**Inner midfielder (IM)**
- *Offensive*, more Passing, much less Defending, somewhat less Playmaking, somewhat more Scoring.
- *Defensive*, more Defending, less Passing, somewhat less Playmaking, somewhat less Scoring.
- *Toward wing*, a nice amount of Winger, somewhat less Playmaking, somewhat more side Passing/Defending, somewhat less central Passing/Defending, no Scoring.

**Forward (FW)**
- *Defensive* (= DF), somewhat more Playmaking, more Passing (Technical forwards get even more side Passing), much less Scoring, somewhat less Winger.
- *Toward wing* (= FTW), more Winger, much more side Scoring/Passing, much less central Scoring/Passing, somewhat less off-side Scoring/Winger/Passing, somewhat less Playmaking.

Key takeaways for tactical guidance:
- Wing back "toward middle" turns a WB into a quasi-CD with a Playmaking lift, useful against AIM opponents.
- CD "toward wing" turns a CD into a quasi-WB without losing all central coverage, useful against AOW opponents.
- IM "toward wing" gives no Scoring, do not pair it with PIC unless you accept losing central attack entirely.
- FW Defensive (DF) is the textbook setup for a "playmaking forward" that drops to feed wing attacks, FW Toward wing (FTW) is the "wide forward" that becomes a second winger on one side.

## Worked Example (Unwritten Manual)

Player skills:
- Goalkeeper: Disastrous (1)
- Defending: Solid (7)
- Playmaking: Excellent (8)
- Winger: Excellent (8)
- Passing: Inadequate (5)
- Scoring: Passable (6)

Played as **RCD (Right Central Defender Normal)** in a 4-4-2 with 2 CDs, his absolute (HO) contribution is:
- Left defence: DE(7) × 0.00 = 0
- Right defence: DE(7) × 0.503 = 3.521 (Disastrous High)
- Central defence: DE(7) × 0.602 = 4.214 (Disastrous Very High)
- Midfield: PM(8) × 0.113 = 0.904 (Disastrous Very Low — anything 0.001–1.999 = Disastrous Very Low)
- Attack lines: 0 (CDs don't contribute to attack)
